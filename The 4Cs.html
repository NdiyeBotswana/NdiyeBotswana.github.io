The 4C's 

The 4 C’s are a simple way for graders and sorters to determine the value of a diamond. The 4 C’s are CARAT, CLARITY, COLOR and CUT. It’s the combination of all 4 C’s that create the monetary value of the diamonds we love, so it is important to find a stone with the right combination to best fit your needs and budget.

CLARITY: The purer a stone is, the greater value it has.
 
Clarity refers to how clear the stones is. Almost every diamond has some type of inclusion and this is what create feature for every stone. The most inclusions are not visible to the naked eye and can only be identified with a x10 diamond loupe. Diamonds are grouped according to the levels of inclusion. Flawless stones are the rarest stones and are the top of color grading scale (FL –IF). Stones with minor inclusions are VVS1 and VVS2. With more visible inclusions, the stones are grade lower as seen in the figure. 

Carat: The bigger the size, the rarer the stone is.

Large stones are rare specimens in nature; therefore the larger the stone creates more value. As we saw in 2017, Botswana uncovered Lesedi wa Rona which was the second biggest rough diamond ever discovered in history. Although the size of a stone makes a diamond more desirable, contributing factors such as color, clarity and cut can greatly affect the overall value of the stone.

Color: The Clearer the better

Diamonds are graded according to color on a letter scale that starts with D and continues down the alphabet. D graded stones are completely colorless and the most valuable stones. Stones that are in the lower letters are usually tinted with yellow or brown and have a lower value. On the other end of the alphabet are Z+ stones which are called Fancy stones. These stones are appear as red, pink, blue, green and yellow. 

Cut: A beautiful jewel is a diamond cut right.
 
When a rough diamond is cut well, it refers to the diamonds ability to reflect colour and light with a remarkable brilliance.
