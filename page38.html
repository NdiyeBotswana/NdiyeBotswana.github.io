From Tawana pre-school to the University of Cape Town

OABILE MOGOMELO

Writer: Ndiye Ngubula
Position: Clarity founder and Director

We know diamond towns for generating diamonds in Botswana but here at Clarity we want to show appreciation to the individuals who grew up in these towns.  

Oabile Mogomelo is an Orapa native who spent most of his child hood growing up in the mining town. If the surname Mogomelo rings a bell, it would be due to his Grand mother (Mma Mogomelo) who worked at the Shopping Center.

Oabile did his primary education at Livingstone house and later on did his secondary at Maru-a-pula. After secondary he made the hard career choice we all face, “What should we study?”.  

When I asked what went through his mind at this stage, he replied with the following,

“ I've always been good with Mathematics, Physics and Chemistry and during form 5 a lot of my friends who went abroad went for Chemistry Engineering. So I did a little research on it and found it interesting. Then I found out my own cousin actually was a chemical engineer in Orapa(I was doing A levels at this point). I was told it was a challenge and that just sold it for me coz I love a challenge.”  

He later om moved to Cape Town to pursue his dream of becomig a chemical engineer. Just like most people who grew up in minning towns, Oabile has a love for sports. He picked up golfing as a stressing relieving hobbie during his time at UCT. He choose golfing because it reminded him of Orapa. 

With his heart set on becoming a chemical engineer, Oabile graduated from the University of Cape Town (UCT).
