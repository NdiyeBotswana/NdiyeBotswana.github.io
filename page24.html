BLOOD, SWEAT AND UNITY

39 years in the mine

The years may pass but the memories will always playback like you’re in the moment. My journey in the Diamond mines in Orapa started in 1970. Back then whenever we entered the mines in the morning for work it wasn’t because we wanted a fancy meal on our tables at the end of the month, we did it because we knew that our duties would change the direction of our country’s development. Little did I know that our efforts would change how the world views Botswana.

When I started off in Orapa, I was part of the geometeor team. After a couple years I became a mine planner. My job was to make sure that annual targets were met. This meant if they wanted 30 million carats at the end of the year, our only response would be to splice more than a 100 million tones of kimberlite into different categories.


Just like any other form of employment, this wasn’t a straight forward operation. We had to consider weather, flights and times. It was hard work but the environment and people made the work worthwhile. looking back on it now all I can do is smile at every moment.

Writer: Ignatius Munyadzwe
Position:  Mine planner 

Mr Munyadzwe spent 39 years working in the Orapa mine as Meteorologist and was later promoted to a mine planner.
