DIFFERENT TYPES OF ROUGH DIAMONDS

Polished diamonds come in different shapes and sizes but not everyone knows what they look like when they are discovered. As we know, diamonds in Botswana are discovered in Kimberlite (If you don’t know follow this link to learn about diamond exploration).

Within the kimberlite, rough diamonds are discovered with four crystalized shapes. 1) Octahedron, 2 Dodecahedron, 3) Cube and 4) Rhombododecahedron. Diamonds crystallize in a cubic system and it’s crystalline shapes are numerous but the basic forms are the shapes stated above.

Once the have been KPC certified and received, then they are sorted at DTCB into three categories: 1) Gem Quality, 2) Industrial Quality and 3) Crushing-Boart or Boart. 


1) GEM QUALITY DIAMONDS

Gem Quality stones are used by the jewelry industy. These stones must have a color and superior clarity to be regard as a Gem Stone. The shape of the diamond is not as important because the diamond must be cut and polished. Gem stones have 15 classification subcategories.

1) Stones, 2)Fancy Colors 3)Closed stones 4) Spotted stones, 5) Naats 6)Irregular stones, 7) Cleavable stones, 8) Coated stones, 9) Frosted stones, 10) Milky stones 11) Blocks,12) Plats 13) Sands 14) Commons Goods, 14) Rejections Stones 

2) INDUSTRIAL DIAMONDS

Industrial diamonds are reserved for industrial use. These stones still require to be of good quality for use. Yellow diamonds are favored because they tend to be stronger than other stones. Industrial stones are classified according to their weight, lower than 3 carats, and also depending on their number of points. These are used in manufacturing drill tool etc.

3) Crushing-Boart 


Boart diamonds are bad quality stones. They are crushed and turned into diamond dust which is used for polishing the other stones.
